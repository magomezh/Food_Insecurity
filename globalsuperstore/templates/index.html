Methodology

Our research explores if the presence or distance to a food bank is a predictor of food insecurity on the county level. We began our analysis by retrieving food bank location (web-scraping) and food insecurity rate data (by online request) from the nonprofit organization Feeding America. Two data frames were created to store the datasets received, in which one contained foodbank county location data and another food insecurity rates.

Our foodbank location data frame only included zip codes, so we had to find the corresponding FIPS (unique county identifier) to each zip code to merge with the food insecurity data frame. Once both data frames joined, we enriched our dataset by ingesting distance between counties data, provided by the National Bureau of Economic Research. Next, our final data frame was created to support our geo-temporal analysis and to contain extra features such as unemployment rate, household income, poverty rate, and education.
